Kehua Culture and Education Foundation

Kehua Culture and Education Foundation was founded in 2017 by Clare Chen, aiming to develop the environment and education for arts, and promote international cultural exchange.

Kehua Culture and Education Foundation consists two main branches â€“ Project M, and Vocal Asia. Project M focuses on organizing a cappella events for the remote areas, including concerts, workshops, lectures, a music camps for youth and teachers. Vocal Asia focuses on establishing a platform for a cappella organizations around the globe, and organize the annual Vocal Asia Festival, which started in 2011, traveling to different cities around Asia each year, making it the biggest and most influential a cappella festival in Asia.

We believe music sends out messages beyond words. Different genres of music being sung by the purity of human voices can inspire more connections between the musicians and the listeners. Through a cappella, we wish to spread the goodness and harmony of humanity, and to connect different cultures and races. Kehua also aims to take this form of art into remote areas of Taiwan. With the promoting lectures and training programs, we hope to establish a new hope for Taiwan!

We see ourselves as music educators and promoters, planting the seeds of music, and nurture it into a beautiful flower.
